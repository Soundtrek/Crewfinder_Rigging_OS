# Crewfinder Laws — Rigging OS Adaptation

This document applies the Crewfinder Founder Laws specifically to the Rigging OS system.

---

## LAW 1 — Validate With Reality
If it is not used in the real world, it does not exist.

### Rigging OS Interpretation
- Every feature must be usable during load-in, build, or strike
- No feature exists purely for theoretical completeness
- If riggers will not use it under time pressure, it should not be built

---

## LAW 2 — Protect the Core Loop
Find → Connect → Assign → Work

### Rigging OS Interpretation
- This tool exists inside the WORK phase
- It must connect to jobs, companies, and crew
- It must not become a disconnected tool island

---

## LAW 3 — Build Systems, Not Features

### Rigging OS Interpretation
- No hardcoded safety rules
- No fixed equipment lists
- No static assumptions
- All logic must be configurable via admin/company settings where applicable

---

## LAW 4 — Safety Over Convenience

### Rigging OS Interpretation
- Unsafe outputs must be clearly flagged
- The UI must never make unsafe conditions look acceptable
- Warnings must be visible and explicit
- No silent corrections of dangerous inputs

---

## LAW 5 — Explainability Required

### Rigging OS Interpretation
- Every calculation must include reasoning
- Outputs must be traceable to inputs
- Safety warnings must explain WHY they exist

---

## LAW 6 — No Overengineering

### Rigging OS Interpretation
- MVP must focus on core calculations and workflow
- Avoid building CAD-level complexity
- Build incrementally with real-world validation

---

## LAW 7 — Compliance Support, Not Legal Authority

### Rigging OS Interpretation
- The system may support safety file workflows
- The system must NOT claim legal compliance automatically
- Responsibility remains with the user and appointed competent persons

---

## LAW 8 — Mobile First, Always

### Rigging OS Interpretation
- Must work on-site on a phone
- Large touch targets
- Fast interaction (<1 second feedback where possible)

---

## LAW 9 — Audit Everything Important

### Rigging OS Interpretation
- Calculations must be stored with inputs and outputs
- Job changes must be traceable
- Signoffs must be recorded with timestamps and identity

---

## LAW 10 — Never Mislead on Safety

### Rigging OS Interpretation
- No green status unless clearly safe within defined parameters
- Must distinguish between:
  - mathematically safe
  - policy-compliant
  - verified on-site

---

## Enforcement
These laws are not guidelines.
They are non-negotiable constraints on the system design.
